## What is MacFossils? 🕵️‍♂️

MacFossils helps you find leftover files from apps you have already uninstalled on your Mac. These leftover files take up space and can slow your system over time. This simple tool scans your Mac and shows you what files you can safely remove to free up disk space. It works quietly in the background and gives you control over what gets deleted.

MacFossils focuses on spotting unused leftover files from various applications. It also works well with Apple Silicon Macs and recent macOS versions like Sonoma and Monterey.

---

## Why Use MacFossils? 🧹

When you uninstall software, some files may remain on your Mac without your knowledge. These files include caches, preferences, and support files. Over time, these remnants build up and consume valuable disk space.

MacFossils makes cleaning these files easy. It helps you:

- Identify leftover files from uninstalled apps.
- Remove unnecessary data safely.
- Improve system performance by freeing disk space.
- Maintain your Mac with low effort.

## How to Use MacFossils 🔍

### Step 1: Start a Scan

Click the "Start Scan" button in the app. MacFossils will scan your hard drive to find leftover files from apps you no longer have.

The scan may take several minutes depending on your disk size.

### Step 2: Review Results

When the scan finishes, MacFossils shows a list of leftover app files grouped by app name or type.

You can look through the list to see what files are found. MacFossils will tell you the size of each file or folder.

### Step 3: Select Files to Delete

You choose which leftovers to remove. By default, MacFossils selects files that are safe to delete.

Uncheck any files you want to keep.

### Step 4: Clean Up

Click the “Delete Selected” button to remove these files. You may need to enter your Mac password for permission.

Once done, MacFossils will confirm the cleanup.

## Troubleshooting Tips 🛠️

- If the app won’t open, check your Security & Privacy settings in System Preferences. Allow MacFossils to run.
- If the scan fails partway, try restarting your Mac and run the app again.
- Make sure you have enough free disk space when deleting large files.
- If you see permission errors, run MacFossils again after entering your admin password.
- For slow scans, close other apps and wait patiently.
